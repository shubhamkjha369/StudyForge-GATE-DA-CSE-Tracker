📅 10-Month Study Roadmap — GATE DA 2027

Detailed phase-wise plan from April 2026 to February 2027. Each phase builds on the previous one.

📚 Key Resources
📖 Mathematics: NPTEL Linear Algebra, Kreyszig for Engineering Math
📖 Statistics: Sheldon Ross — Probability & Statistics; Wackerly textbook
📖 ML/AI: NPTEL ML (IIT M), Bishop PRML, Fast.ai for intuition
📖 Programming: Geeks for Geeks DSA, Python DS Handbook
📖 PYQs: MadeEasy GATE DA book + 2023/2024 official papers
📖 Practice: GATE Overflow (DA section), Testbook, Unacademy
🎥 YouTube: NPTEL (Probability & Statistics, Linear Algebra), StatQuest, 3Blue1Brown (essence of LA), Sentdex (Python ML)
🎯 Exam Strategy
⚡ High-weight units: Prob & Stats (15%), ML (15%), Linear Algebra (12%)
⚡ Don't skip: Calculus & Optimization — forms base for ML derivations
⚡ Coding section: Solve 5–10 PYQ coding problems per week in Python
⚡ Weekly mock tests: Start from Month 6 onwards
⚡ Last 3 months: Full PYQ coverage + 2 full mocks/week
📊 Topic weightage (approx):
Prob & Stats 15% ML 15% Linear Algebra 12% Calculus 10% Programming 10% DSA 10% AI 10% DBMS 8%
⏱️ Daily Study Plan Template
6:00–8:00Core theory (new topic from tracker) 8:30–10:00Practice problems / PYQs on yesterday's topic 11:00–12:30Secondary subject or weak area revision 3:00–5:00Coding + implementation practice (Python) 6:00–7:00Mock test / previous year paper 8:00–9:00Review notes + update tracker ✅
💡 Use 25-min Pomodoros for each block. Link them to GATE DA in the Pomodoro timer!
🏆 GATE DA — Key Differences from GATE CS
🔵 GATE DA is a separate paper launched in 2023, distinct from GATE CS
🔵 Heavy focus on Mathematics, Statistics, and ML — 50%+ of marks
🔵 Includes a coding section (programming + DS questions)
🔵 No Digital Electronics, Computer Organization in DA syllabus
🔵 Overlap with GATE CS: DBMS, Algorithms, OS (lighter coverage in DA)
🔵 AI section covers Search, CSP, Bayesian Networks — unique to DA
⚠️ GATE DA 2024 had 65 questions (55 MCQ + 10 coding). Prepare Python heavily!
